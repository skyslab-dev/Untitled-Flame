# Untitled Flame

A quiet, one-life game about a candle riding a wandering colossus through a world that becomes real wherever the player shines their light.

## Core idea

- The world begins as darkness and possibility.
- Illuminating something makes that version of the world permanent.
- The game notices what the player seeks, avoids, and lingers beside.
- Those patterns create the final place reached by the colossus.
- When the flame's life ends, the world closes and leaves behind one final image of the journey.

## First prototype

Build a short 10-minute version containing only:

1. A candle and a slowly moving colossus.
2. A controllable cone of light.
3. Three possible landmarks hidden in darkness.
4. A choice where illuminating one landmark makes it real and removes the others.
5. A final scene assembled from the player's choices.

The prototype should answer one question: **does choosing what deserves light feel meaningful?**

## Not yet

No 500-hour clock, permanent save lock, procedural world, combat, inventory, crafting, dialogue system, or finished art. Those come only after the central light-and-attention mechanic is enjoyable.

## Decisions to make next

- Engine: Godot is the current default candidate.
- Presentation: 2D side view or top-down.
- Art direction: pixel art or hand-drawn silhouettes.
- Final lifespan: real-world timer or playtime-only timer.
